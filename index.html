My name is Michelle Sanchez and I am a 1st year psychology major at UCR. I have been gathering experience and knowledge from various places since a young age from community service and internships whenever the opportunity was presented to me.

One particular internship was in the Community Services Department at the City of Lake Elsinore where I participated in a City Hall meeting and introduced a new agenda that was approved and built within the next couple of years. I also worked as a lifeguard where I learned skills to help in an emergency situation.

Apart from being involved in the city, I've also been involved at my local Elsinore Valley Pregnancy Resource Center (EVPRC) for several years. The resource center provides counsel and resources for pregnant women, families in need, or anyone that walks in. Out of many cases, those that have resonated the most in my mind are those in which I could help the family in a form in which others had previously helped me. For example, many families come in that speak only Spanish, and most members of the EVPRC can't communicate with them. Therefore, as someone bilingual, I can help them obtain what they need.
